Who we are


"He who opens a school door, closes a prison." ~ Victor Hugo

iGen Education Trust is a non-profit organisation whose primary objective is to make higher education in South Africa accessible and equitable for every qualifying student. This will ensure that quality education becomes a reality for ALL university students in South Africa regardless of race, gender, social status, economic background, religion or political affiliation.

Our desire is to give each student an equal chance to realize their full potential in life and contribute towards an improved quality of life for themselves and their families. In the long term, we see ourselves influencing the design and delivery of education in South Africa. We believe high quality, purpose-driven education can transform students into citizens who are able to contribute meaningfully in the provision of solutions needed to address social, political and economic challenges societies face today.



We believe


  • In the power of education to catalyse social and economic change.

  • By enabling equitable education, we can close the gaps that separate students on the basis of affordability.

  • Quality tertiary education is vital to sustaining our democracyand can strengthen South Africa.


Our promise


  • We promise to endeavour to serve every South African child who deserves to be at a tertiary institution and needs funding, irrespective of cultural background.

  • We promise to be truthful in the manner in which we conduct ourselves and deliver on our mandate.

  • We promise to not compromise on integrity, excellence and who we are.

Board of Trustees

iGen Education Trust is committed to good governance which will promote the organisation’s vision, strengthen the Board of Trustees (The Board) and management accountability and build public trust. The primary role and responsibility of The Board is to retain full, efficient and effective control of The Trust, and serve as a decision-making hub and custodian of corporate governance in the organisation. It will also act in the best interest of the Trust and its Stakeholders at all times and serve as the Trust guardian of values and ethics.

The Board is responsible for strategic direction, policy, boundaries and oversight; monitoring performance and ensuring sound financial management. The Trust’s governance framework supports the principles of integrity, strong ethical values (responsibility, accountability, fairness and transparency), diversity and excellence.

The Board and management recognise and acknowledge that iGen Education Trust is a registered Non-Profit Organisation (NPO) and is therefore accountable to donors and stakeholders. As a result, The Board will ensure that The Trust is annually audited and appropriate financial statements are prepared (in line with the requirements of the NPO Act) to the standards of generally accepted accounting practice.

Funding

As a start, only students accepted at any of the 26 universities in South Africa will be covered.


Who can apply

Any student who has been accepted into an accredited South African university but either lacks or does not have sufficient means to pay for their studies.

What the funding covers

Registration, tuition, prescribed books, approved study materials, accommodation and meals. Funding is automatically renewed with every progressive year of study.


How the funding is awarded

Applications are processed by universities on the basis of academic ability and financial need of the applicant. The scholarship is not repayable.


How to apply

Submit your application for funding together with your application to study to your tertiary institution.


Support iGen


Why should I donate?

Your generous donation goes towards funding students in need, making sure we make South African tertiary education accessible and equitable to all students. Almost all universities in South Africa are battling with high student debts and thus do not have adequate funds to maximise the impact of their offerings. By March 2019, students were owing different universities over R9 billion and we hope to assist in reducing this gap, and in eliminating it.

By donating at least R100 every month to iGen Education Trust, you can impact not just students but families and generations.

Corporate Donations

Corporates can support our mission once-off, regularly or monthly. Whatever your choice is, a Section 18A certificate will be issued to your organisation, on request.


Where will my money go?

All the funds collected go to The iGen Education Trust. Eighty percent (80%) goes directly to student fees and one percent (1%) covers operational costs. The rest, nineteen percent (19%), is used for comprehensive marketing to drive fund growth.
